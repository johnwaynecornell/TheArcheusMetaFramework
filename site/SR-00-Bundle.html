SR-00-01 ‚Äî Symbolic Rhyme (Structure)

Purpose: To give symbolic rhyme a structured basis: categories, patterns, and forms. This extends SR-00 (Seed) by making rhyme legible as a repeatable operator rather than a one-off insight.

1) CONTEXT

SR-00 defined symbolic rhyme as the resonance of opposites across domains. Here, we begin structuring that resonance: what kinds of rhymes exist, what shapes they take, and how they can be carried forward.

2) TYPOLOGY OF RHYME

  • Direct Rhyme: Simple pairing (X ‚Üî Y) within a domain.
  • Cross Rhyme: Crossing functions (X frames what Y imbues; Y frames what X imbues).
  • Extended Rhyme: Long-form metaphor sustained across multiple domains.
  • Recursive Rhyme: Rhyme that refers back to its own pairing (self-symbolic echo).

SR-00-03 ‚Äî Symbolic Rhyme (Canon)

Purpose: To articulate how symbolic rhyme passes from resonance into canon. At this stage, rhymes endure not only as patterns or resonances but as shared references and cultural anchors ‚Äî principles recognized and repeated across generations.

1) CONTEXT

Canon emerges when resonance stabilizes into reference. A rhyme that once surprised becomes familiar; what was once metaphor becomes doctrine, proverb, axiom, or symbol. This canonization allows rhyme to shape practice and tradition, serving as a point of orientation in thought and culture.

2) DIMENSIONS OF CANON

  • Cultural Canon: Rhymes absorbed into collective memory (e.g., Yin ‚Üî Yang).
  • Philosophical Canon: Pairings elevated into enduring principles (e.g., Freedom ‚Üî Responsibility).
  • Scientific Canon: Rhyme-like formulations that anchor paradigms (e.g., Wave ‚Üî Particle duality).
  • Symbolic Canon: Archetypal rhymes enshrined in FAR/Archeus frameworks for recursive use.

Thus, the FAR suite positions canon not as closure, but as a living repository ‚Äî principles meant to be re-echoed, re-interpreted, and re-imbued.
