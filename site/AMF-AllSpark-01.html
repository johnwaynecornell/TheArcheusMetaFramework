4. AMF Thought Stabilization Methods

While feedback loops enhance cognition, they must be:

  1. Directed (Avoiding infinite feedback recursion).

4.1. Preventing Echo Chambers

  • Echo chambers occur when feedback loops reinforce bias rather than refine clarity.
  • AMF-AllSpark introduces counterbalancing edges to challenge weak thought structures.

Solution:

  • Introduce divergent feedback pathways.
  • Enforce cross-domain connections to expand symbolic perspectives.

4.2. Ensuring Thought Adaptability

  • If thought structures become rigid, innovation stalls.
  • MCF introduces controlled perturbations to test adaptive robustness.

Example:

  • Thought A ‚Üí B ‚Üí C stabilizes.
  • MCF injects a controlled alternative: D (New Perspective).
